Quarterly Communications Plan

One quarter, five beats.

A 3-month cycle that repeats four times a year. Same shape every quarter — partner check-ins, an event, an educational post, a quarterly close. Predictable enough to run without a meeting; specific enough to feel personal every time.

← Back to project progress

The Shape

Why a quarterly cycle.

A 12-month plan looks ambitious in a slide and dies in week six. A 3-month cycle is short enough to remember and long enough to land — and because it repeats verbatim every quarter, it gets easier each pass. Four cycles a year means four chances to show up consistently with the same voice, the same beats, on the same surfaces.

Roughly 20 touches across the year, all on-brand, all repeatable. Nothing here is a campaign. The plan is the campaign.

The Five Beats

What lands in each quarter.

Every quarter, the same five beats hit in the same order. Numbering is the order they ship, not their importance.

  1. 01
    Month 1 · Week 1

    Quarterly opener.

    A short email to the full list — clients past and present, referral partners, agents. Plain language, signed by name. One thing the team is paying attention to this quarter, one thing in the market, one practical note. No newsletter design. The point is the note, not the layout.

    • Email
    • Personal sign-off
  2. 02
    Month 1 · Weeks 3–4

    Partner check-ins.

    Personalized texts to the top 10–15 referral relationships. Not a blast — one human reading a name, sending a sentence. Asks for nothing. Mentions one specific thing about that person's recent work. This is the relational engine; it earns the rest of the year.

    • Text / SMS
    • Hand-written when warranted
  3. 03
    Month 2 · Mid-month

    Third Thursday — the touchpoint.

    Hosted in person. The investment is the host showing up — pre-event personal texts to attendees, day-of presence, day-after follow-up. No swag, no kit, no printed program. The content of the event is the relationship. Treat it as the most important beat of the quarter.

    • In-person
    • Pre + post text follow-up
  4. 04
    Month 2 · End of month → Month 3 · Mid-month

    Educational post.

    One useful piece of writing, distributed three ways: a Google Business Profile post, a short LinkedIn note, and a page on the site for search to index. Topic is something an agent or buyer just asked the team. Plain answer, on the record, ranked. One per quarter; four per year.

    • Google Business Profile
    • LinkedIn
    • Website / search
  5. 05
    Month 3 · Final week

    Quarter-close note.

    A brief recap to closed clients from the quarter — a thank-you, a Google review prompt where appropriate, and the offer to be a resource going forward. Quiet, personal, not transactional. Closes the loop on the relationship the closing started.

    • Email
    • Review prompt where genuine
Year View

How four quarters add up.

Four cycles, identical shape. Each quarter contributes the same five beats; the names and faces inside them rotate as the business moves through the year.

Beat Q1 Q2 Q3 Q4
01 Quarterly opener Jan W1 Apr W1 Jul W1 Oct W1
02 Partner check-ins Jan W3–4 Apr W3–4 Jul W3–4 Oct W3–4
03 Third Thursday Feb May Aug Nov
04 Educational post Feb–Mar May–Jun Aug–Sep Nov–Dec
05 Quarter-close note Mar W4 Jun W4 Sep W4 Dec W4

Twenty touches a year. Every one on-brand. None of them require a campaign meeting.

Operating Notes

How to actually run it.

  • Owner. One person owns the calendar. Not a committee. The owner sends the openers, drafts the educational post, and reminds whoever is running Third Thursday two weeks out.
  • Drafts live in one place. A single shared doc per quarter holds the opener, the educational post, and the close note in draft. Reviewed once mid-month-1; published on the dates above.
  • Texts aren't drafted. Beat 02 and the Third Thursday personal touches do not get scripted. If they read like a template, they fail. The owner blocks 90 minutes on the calendar; sends them human.
  • Educational topic comes from the inbox. Whatever question came up most that quarter from agents and buyers becomes the post. Don't invent topics.
  • Measure two things. Reply rate on the opener (qualitative — are people writing back?), and Google reviews left in the quarter. Everything else is noise.

Attorney-led property settlements, steady and thorough. The way closings ought to feel.
